Somali Canjeero

Description

Canjeero(Anjera) is the most common breakfast food in Somaliaand Somalians typically eat around 3 or 4 for breakfast, often accompanied by a cup of tea. It is commonly served drizzled with butter or ghee and sugar. It is also accompanied by a curry, meat stew(maraq), soup, or liver and onions. In Somalia they will cook the pancake on a tava which is circular, disc stove used frequently just for cooking canjeero(Anjera). However, since most Westerners are unlikely to have a tava lying around, a conventional pan will suffice for making this recipe.

Ingredients

  • White maize flour
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Yeast

Steps to take to cook

  1. 1 cup of white maize flour.
  2. 2 cup of flour.
  3. 1 tablespoon of salt.
  4. 4 tablespoon of sugar.
  5. 1 tablespoon of yeast.
  6. Water.
  7. Mix it well (prepare it at night since it needs to be fermented and cook it early in the morning).
