Somali Sambusa

Description

Somali Sambusas are a stuffed pastry which is deep fried. The dough for the pastry is made from wheat flour. It is rolled out into a large circle then cut down the middle into 4 pieces. Each piece is folded into a cone, and while sitting in the palm of the hand, filled. The top part of the cone is folded down giving the sambusa its signature traingle shape. While sambusa can be filled with vegetables, the most popular type is filled with meat. Sambusa is similar to the indian snack Samosa. It is a very popular appetizer served not only during Ramadan, but any on occasion as an appetizer.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef/lamp.
  • Flour.
  • Onion
  • Coriander powder.
  • Green Chilli.
  • Salt.
  • Clove garlic.

Steps to take to make Sambusa

  1. Cook the meat in a skillet with a little oil on a medium heat
  2. Add the onions and green chilli, stir until tender. Then add salt, and pepper, garlic. Cook briefly for few minutes.
  3. Pick one the sambusa wrappers with your hands and fold it into a cone shape seal it with flour paste
  4. Fill the cone with the filling and close the top into a traingle shape and seal it with the flour paste. Pinch the edges so that it is completely sealed.
  5. continue filling the rest of the sambusas.
  6. Heat the oil in frying pan, fry the sambusas until golden brown.
